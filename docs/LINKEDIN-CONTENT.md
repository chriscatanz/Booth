### Post 5: The Lead Quality Problem

I used to celebrate getting 200 leads at a show.

Then I learned to count differently:

200 badge scans
- 80 competitors and vendors (bye)
- 45 "just browsing" (never respond to follow-up)
- 30 wrong company size
- 25 wrong industry
- 15 already customers

= 5 actual prospects

That's $3,000 per qualified lead on a $15,000 show.

Now I track two numbers:
1. Qualified leads (fits ICP, has budget, has timeline)
2. Cost per qualified lead

Some "bad" shows became great when I looked at quality.
Some "great" shows became terrible.

Vanity metrics are expensive.

### Post 7: Feature Spotlight - Shipping Deadlines

The most expensive mistake in trade shows:

Missing a shipping deadline.

Advance warehouse cutoffs are usually 2-3 weeks before the show.

Miss it? You're paying:
- Rush shipping ($$$)
- Direct-to-show rates ($$$$)
- Or worse: your booth arrives late

I've seen companies spend an extra $5,000 because someone forgot a date.

That's why Booth shows shipping deadlines front and center.

Not buried in a spreadsheet. Not in someone's calendar.

Right there. Every time you look at a show.
